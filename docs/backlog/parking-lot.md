# BasketSense parking lot

These ideas are intentionally outside the ordered backlog. Parking an idea is
not a judgment that it is permanently bad; it means current evidence does not
justify its cost, risk, or distraction.

## Deferred ideas

- Public self-service launch.
- Hundreds of active households before friend-beta evidence.
- Unsupported Costco login automation or credential storage.
- Browser scraping or extensions.
- Email receipt forwarding.
- Cross-household collaborative filtering.
- Black-box recommendation models.
- LLM-calculated totals or accounting decisions.
- Pantry inventory inferred solely from purchase cadence.
- Native mobile applications.
- Per-household D1 databases.
- Generic budgeting features unrelated to the weekly household loop.

## Rule for reconsideration

An item may leave the parking lot only when new evidence identifies a household
problem, the ordered backlog cannot solve it more simply, privacy and data
requirements are understood, and Harsh and Codex explicitly agree on its new
priority and decision-gate impact.
