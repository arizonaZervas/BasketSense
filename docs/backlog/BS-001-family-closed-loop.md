# BS-001 — Prove the family closed loop

**Status:** Ready  
**Priority:** 1  
**Decision gate:** Gate A

## Outcome

Harsh and Navni use BasketSense across four real Saturday cycles and produce
enough evidence to decide whether the product reduces weekly planning effort
without making the Costco ritual less enjoyable.

## Rationale

Multi-tenancy would multiply an unproven workflow. Four repeated cycles test
whether BasketSense remains useful after novelty fades and expose the bugs that
matter more than speculative features.

## Dependencies

- The current private family deployment remains available.
- Urgent weekly-loop bugs may interrupt all other backlog work.
- No external household is invited during this item.

## Smallest test

Complete one full cycle—plan, freeze, shop, ingest, reconcile, answer review
questions, and inspect the updated dashboard—then repeat the same observation
for four Saturdays without changing the measurement definitions.

## Evidence to record each week

- Whether both spouses reviewed or used the shared list.
- Planning and list-review minutes.
- Suggested items accepted, removed, and retained at freeze.
- Receipt ingestion and correction minutes.
- Any arithmetic, product, category, or planned-versus-actual error.
- Duplicate, waste, regret, or satisfying-discovery feedback actually provided.
- A short enjoyment check: better, unchanged, or worse than shopping without
  BasketSense.
- Bugs, confusing moments, and workarounds.

## Acceptance criteria

- Four complete weekly records exist.
- Every finalized receipt reconciles before affecting exact metrics.
- Dashboard totals and product histories remain accurate after each cycle.
- Recommendation usefulness and false-positive burden can be discussed from
  observed outcomes, not memory alone.
- Harsh and Navni can make a Gate A decision together.

## Privacy risks

Weekly notes contain household spending and behavioral feedback. Keep raw notes
private, record only what changes a product decision, and do not publish receipt
or purchase details in issue trackers.

## Explicit non-goals

- Recruiting another household.
- Optimizing for scale.
- Treating lower spending as the sole success measure.
