# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

BasketSense is a private, household-first Costco companion for a married couple who plan and shop together, usually on a mobile phone around their weekly Saturday trip. It should make coordination easier without turning an enjoyable family ritual into financial surveillance.

## Product Purpose

Help the household remember recurring essentials, make deliberate room for worthwhile discoveries, understand where Costco spending goes, and learn from each trip. Success means a useful shared Saturday list, less duplicate or regretted buying, trustworthy receipt-backed insights, and a trip that still feels enjoyable.

## Positioning

A shared household learning loop that connects pre-trip intent, Costco receipts, and lightweight feedback so every Saturday list becomes more useful.

## Brand Personality

Helpful, encouraging, and calm. The product should feel like a thoughtful household companion: specific enough to be useful, warm without being cute, and honest about what the data can and cannot prove.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a restrictive budget enforcer, a guilt-based savings app, a spouse-monitoring tool, or a generic business-intelligence dashboard. Avoid presenting receipt-derived guesses as facts.

## Design Principles

- Preserve the enjoyable Costco ritual while making planning easier.
- Keep one shared household view that either spouse can understand and edit.
- Make every recommendation explainable, optional, and easy to reject.
- Separate recorded intent from post-trip outcomes before describing a purchase as unplanned.
- Show uncertain receipt and category mappings as reviewable uncertainty, not hidden precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design mobile-first for quick use at home and in a busy warehouse. Use clear language, visible focus states, comfortable reading sizes, and touch targets large enough for reliable one-handed use. Respect reduced-motion preferences and never depend on color or hover alone to communicate meaning.
